About The Business Permit Licensing Office

The Business Permit Licensing Office (BPLO) is responsible for regulating business activities within the jurisdiction. We ensure that businesses comply with local regulations and ordinances.

Our mission is to foster a business-friendly environment while maintaining order and safety for the community. Through innovation and technology, we aim to make government services more accessible to business owners.

The BPLO processes applications for new businesses, handles renewals, amendments, and manages business closure procedures. We work closely with other government agencies to streamline the permitting process.

Required Documents

Prepare these documents before starting your application to ensure a smooth process.

Business Registration

DTI Certificate for sole proprietorship, SEC Registration for corporations, or CDA Registration for cooperatives.

Proof of Location

Contract of Lease, Title, or other proof of ownership or right to use the business location.

Valid ID

Government-issued identification of the business owner or authorized representative.

Barangay Clearance

Certification from the local barangay that the business can operate in the area.

Fire Safety Inspection

Certificate from the Bureau of Fire Protection ensuring the safety of the premises.

Premises Photos

Photos of the business premises, including the storefront with signage.

Note: Additional documents may be required depending on your business type and location.
